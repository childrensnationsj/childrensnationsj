The Children's Nation for South Jersey (CNSJ) is an organization determined to protect and ensure the ongoing safety of children and college students of federally, state and locally funded educational institutions and libraries based in the South Jersey area, with a focus on the counties of Gloucester and Cumberland.
1. Federally, state and locally funded educational institutions in the South Jersey area, such as public K-12 schools, community colleges, universities and libraries should not be allowed to disperse or approve literature, student programs, lessons, media or other endeavors that may endanger the mental, psychological or moral well-being of students. All material must be age appropriate for the educational institution.
2. Students and citizens should have access to libraries and media catalogs that are free of literature and other media including, but not limited to, supporting pedophilic, gender confusion and critical race ideology.
3. The process of auditing and reforming educational institutions should be easily accessible to everyday citizens, students and parents of children without obfuscation.
1. Publicly censure, dismantle and remove literature and other forms of media distributed and approved by federally, state and locally funded educational institutions and libraries including, but not limited to, material approving of pedophilic, anti-religious and critical race themes and underlying expressions.
2. Ensure books, lessons and other forms of media and literature needed for required courses for graduation of students of federally, state and locally funded educational institutions do not include the properties as mentioned in Missions [1].
3. Grant students of federally, state and locally funded educational institutions with a reasonable ability to reject material they find holding the properties as mentioned in Missions [1] and accommodations of an immediate reasonable alternative of material.
4. Enfranchise students and parents of children enrolled in federally, state and locally funded educational institutions and libraries alike to decide if literature and other forms of media are allowed into library or media catalogs.
5. Ensure complete, unobstructed and transparent virtual book and media catalogs of all federally, state or locally funded educational institutions and libraries.
Is CNSM affiliated a youth group?

No. CNSJ is a children's advocacy group; we currently do not provide support or activities for children. We do want to hear from the children though! We encourage young South Jersey students in middle school or high school who might want to share concerns about their educational institution to email us (bottom of website).

Is CNSJ affiliated with any political party?

CNSJ is completely independent of any political affiliation with a party, board, union, organization or other entity.

Is banning books against freedom of speech?

Banning books and other resources that are harmful to children is not against freedom of speech. From a legal standpoint, disallowing certain books in public libraries is not a violation of freedom of speech. Obviously, if a book told a student how to make a bomb, it does not belong in a library. We firmly and and completely believe that are erotic, salacious, sexually suggestive or critical race in nature do not belong in young children's libraries.

Why should books be banned from college student adults?

CNSJ, as a part of its third clause in its Core Beliefs, believes adult-aged college students should know and have a say on what books are entering their libraries. Libraries on public colleges and universities are also funded fully or partially by state and local taxes, therefore taxpayers should also be aware of the books being entered into these libraries.

How does CNSJ intend to fulfill its missions?

CNSJ will petition local and state government through peaceful protests, speeches and board meetings. It will engage online activism through its various social media channels by posting excerpts of literature, lessons plans and other approved resources as officially censured by CNSJ. We intend to communicate officially censured books through our social media channels.

How do I get involved with CNSJ?

Thank you for expressing interest in the movement! Please reach out to us through our channels at the bottom of the website and we will reach out to you at our earliest chance.
